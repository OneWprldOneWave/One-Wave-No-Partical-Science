
DEFINITIONS

Core Symbols

φ

Single continuous scalar displacement value at coordinate (n,i).

φ is the fundamental field quantity evolved by the update rule.

---

i

A coordinate location in the fixed-node lattice.

Nodes do not move.

The field value at a node changes.

---

β_i

Coupling strength between node i and neighboring nodes.

β controls how strongly displacement propagates between nodes.

---

γ

Damping term.

Field fatigue or amplitude loss per iteration.

---

c

Friction limit.

Maximum propagation rate through the field.

---

Core Concepts

Displacement

The deviation of φ from local equilibrium within the lattice.

Displacement is the fundamental quantity represented by φ.

The update rule propagates displacement.

All propagation, interference, excitation, localization, mass effects, and observable structures are configurations of displacement.

---

Field

The complete set of displacement values φ across the lattice.

The field is the medium through which displacement propagates.

The field is not composed of particles.

---

Lattice

The fixed-node structure on which φ evolves.

Nodes remain fixed.

Displacement values change through the update rule.

---

Propagation

The transmission of displacement through neighboring lattice couplings.

Propagation is controlled by β and γ and limited by c.

---

Gradient

A spatial difference in displacement or density.

Gradients drive propagation and localization behavior.

---

Density

The local concentration of displacement within a region.

Density may contribute to gradients, localization, and excitation formation.

---

Damping

The loss of displacement amplitude through iteration.

Represented by γ.

---

Friction

The limiting effect that restricts propagation speed.

Represented by c.

---

Resistance

Opposition to displacement propagation through the lattice.

Resistance influences localization, persistence, and mass behavior.

---

Impedance

The slowing of displacement propagation caused by lattice conditions.

Mass is currently interpreted as impedance at β-gradients.

---

Equilibrium

A state in which local displacement differences no longer drive net propagation.

---

Coherence

The persistence of organized displacement structure through successive updates.

A coherent structure maintains identity despite damping, propagation, and interference.

---

Oscillation

Repeated variation of displacement around equilibrium.

---

Interference

The interaction of multiple displacement patterns.

Interference may be constructive or destructive.

---

Localization

The concentration of displacement into a bounded region.

---

Persistence

The continued survival of a displacement structure through successive updates.

---

Excitation

A localized displacement structure distinguishable from the surrounding field.

---

Mode

A stable or semi-stable displacement pattern supported by the lattice.

---

Phase

Relative alignment between displacement patterns.

Phase relationships determine interference behavior.

---

Core Structures

Black Hole

Region where β_i → 0.

Propagation becomes physically impossible.

---

Negative Space

Region where destructive interference locks φ into non-propagating loops.

Distinct from dark matter.

---

Interface

Boundary between differing lattice conditions.

Often represented by a change in β structure.

---

Bound State

A localized displacement structure that remains confined to a region.

---

Winding

Non-trivial displacement structure associated with closed-loop topology.

Current sustaining mechanism remains unresolved under LATTICE_SIG_007.

---

Selection Rule

A rule determining which displacement structures may form or persist.

Currently unresolved.

---

Eigenmode

A displacement pattern naturally supported by lattice geometry.

---

Forbidden Gap

A spectral region in which no stable eigenmode exists.
